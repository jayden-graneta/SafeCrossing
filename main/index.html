About SafeCrossing

On June 21, 2024, my life underwent a drastic change. I was the victim of a hit-and-run while crossing at a yield-to-pedestrians crosswalk. I had just gotten off the bus coming home from the gym when a black minivan swerved around a bus and stopped, hitting me in the middle of the crosswalk. I ended up riding the top of his hood until he finally stopped, where I was then flung onto the ground. As soon as I stood up and realized what had happened, the driver had already driven off. I was rushed to the hospital, where I would have a dime-sized piece of bone from my elbow pulled and told I needed to receive surgery to remove air in my joint and some other medical lingo I didn't understand. All in all, I tore a bunch of muscles in my forearm, received minor surgery on my elbow and had a piece of my elbow pulled out in front of me.

After my accident, I was unable to do any of the things which I once loved, such as going to the gym, coding or even something as simple as walking and eating became a pain.
